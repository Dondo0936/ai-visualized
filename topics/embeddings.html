The Big Idea
Words are numbers in disguise
To do math on words, we turn each word into a list of numbers (a vector). Words with similar meanings end up close together in space. This is learned from reading millions of sentences — words that appear in similar contexts get similar vectors.

Classic Analogy

king - man + woman ≈ queen
This isn't magic — it's vector arithmetic! Subtract "man" from "king" to get a "royalty minus male" direction. Add "woman" and you arrive near "queen". The fact that real embeddings learn this from raw text is one of the most remarkable results in AI.

Real-World Scale

What you see here is 2D. Real embeddings are 768, 1536, or even 4096 dimensions. GPT-3's embeddings are 12,288-dimensional! We squish them down to 2D using techniques like t-SNE or UMAP just to visualize.

How Distance Is Measured

Similarity between two embeddings is usually cosine similarity — the dot product of the unit vectors. Two words point in nearly the same direction = high similarity (near 1.0). Opposite directions = -1.0. Unrelated = near 0.
