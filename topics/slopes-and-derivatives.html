The Big Idea
The derivative = "how fast is it changing right now?"
Imagine driving on a hilly road. Your speedometer doesn't show where you are — it shows how fast you're moving at this instant. The derivative is the speedometer for any function. It tells you the slope at exactly one point.

Intuition Builder

At x = 0, the parabola is at its lowest point. The tangent line is perfectly flat (slope = 0). This means the function is not changing at this instant — it's a minimum!

Try This!

1. On y = x², find where the slope is exactly 0. What's special about that point?
2. Turn on "Secant Line" and shrink Δx toward 0. Watch the secant become the tangent — that's the limit definition of a derivative!
3. On "Hill", find where slope = 0. That's the peak — gradient descent stops here!

Connection to AI

Gradient descent is literally "follow the slope downhill." When training a neural network, we compute the derivative of the loss function, and step in the direction where the slope goes down. The derivative is the engine that makes all AI learning possible.

Key Vocabulary

A secant line connects two points on a curve — it gives the average slope between them. A tangent line touches the curve at exactly one point — it gives the instantaneous slope. The derivative is what you get when you shrink the secant until the two points merge into one.
Where slope = 0, something interesting happens: it could be a minimum (valley bottom), a maximum (hill top), or a saddle point (flat but not a peak or valley). Gradient descent looks for minima but can get tricked by saddle points — try the 3D visualization to see this!
