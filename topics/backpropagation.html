The Big Idea
Training = forward + backward
Forward pass: push inputs through the network, get a prediction, measure the loss. Backward pass: compute how much each weight contributed to the error using the chain rule. Each weight gets a gradient. Gradient descent then nudges every weight in the direction that reduces loss.

The Chain Rule

To find how much weight w affects loss L, we multiply gradients along the path:

∂L/∂w = ∂L/∂y · ∂y/∂h · ∂h/∂w

Each piece is easy (just a local derivative). The product gives you the full gradient. This is what lets us train networks with billions of parameters.

Real Networks

Modern deep learning frameworks (PyTorch, TensorFlow, JAX) do backprop automatically — "autodiff". You write the forward pass; they compute gradients for free. GPT-4 has ~1.7T parameters. Every single one gets a gradient on every training step.
