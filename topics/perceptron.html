Rosenblatt's rule. For each training example (x, y) with y ∈ {−1, +1}, predict ŷ = sign(w·x + b). If ŷ ≠ y, push: w ← w + η y x, b ← b + η y. The weight vector chases the misclassified point; the decision line tilts toward a better split. Novikoff's theorem (1962) guarantees this finishes in finite steps if a separator exists.
