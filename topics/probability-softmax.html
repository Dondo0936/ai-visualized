The Big Idea
From raw scores to probabilities
Neural networks output raw numbers called logits. They can be positive, negative, anything. Softmax turns them into valid probabilities: all positive, all summing to 1. The class with the highest logit gets the highest probability.

Temperature

Low τ = sharp (winner takes all). High τ = flat (even chances). See Softmax Temperature for deep dive.

Softmax Properties

• All outputs are positive (exp always positive)
• All outputs sum to 1 (valid probability distribution)
• Translation-invariant — adding 100 to every logit gives the same result
• Winner-takes-all as τ → 0; uniform as τ → ∞

Why exp()?

Exp makes everything positive AND amplifies differences. A logit 2 larger becomes ~7.4× more likely. This encourages the model to commit to an answer. The "soft" in softmax = a smooth approximation of argmax (which would just pick one).

Used Everywhere

Softmax is the final layer of almost every classifier. In LLMs, it turns per-token logits into the next-word probability distribution — and then temperature controls how "creative" the sampling is.

Formula

softmax(z)i = ezi/τ / Σ ezj/τ
