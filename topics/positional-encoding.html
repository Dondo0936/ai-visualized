The Big Idea
Why transformers need positions
Unlike older models, transformers process all words in parallel. Fast! But they lose track of word order. "Dog bites man" and "Man bites dog" would look identical. Positional encoding adds a unique "signature" to each position so the model knows which word came first.

Why Sine & Cosine?

The original Transformer paper uses sinusoids at different frequencies. Each dimension oscillates at a different rate: dim 0 oscillates fast, dim 64 oscillates slowly. Every position gets a unique pattern, and the model can learn relative distances (like "4 positions apart") using simple linear combinations.

Formula

PE(pos, 2i) = sin(pos / 100002i/d)
PE(pos, 2i+1) = cos(pos / 100002i/d)

Modern Variants

The original sinusoidal PE is being replaced by RoPE (Rotary Position Embeddings) in models like LLaMA and GPT-NeoX. RoPE rotates query/key vectors in 2D planes. The intuition is the same: give each position a distinct pattern that encodes relative distance naturally.
