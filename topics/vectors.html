The Big Idea
A vector is a number with a direction
A regular number (like 5) tells you "how much." A vector tells you "how much" AND "which way." Think of it as an arrow: the length is the magnitude, the direction is... the direction. Velocity, force, and wind are all vectors.

To add vectors, put B's tail at A's tip. The sum goes from A's tail to B's tip. Think of walking: go 3 blocks east, then 2 blocks north — the sum is where you end up.

Dot Product

A · B = 4.00
Angle: 36.9°
The dot product measures how much two vectors point in the same direction.

Positive: same-ish direction
Zero: perpendicular (90°)
Negative: opposite-ish direction

Formula: A · B = AxBx + AyBy

Projection

projBA = (0.0, 0.0)
The projection of A onto B answers: "How much of A goes in B's direction?" It's like casting A's shadow onto B using a flashlight perpendicular to B. This is used everywhere in AI — attention mechanisms compute projections of queries onto keys.

Connection to AI

In neural networks, every neuron computes a dot product: it multiplies its input vector by a weight vector, then sums them up. That's literally A · B. The Perceptron is just: step(weights · input + bias). Word embeddings, attention, and similarity search all use vectors and dot products.
