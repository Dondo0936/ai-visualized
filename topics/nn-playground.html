Key concepts

A single neuron can only draw a line (linear separator). Stack enough non-linear layers and you can draw any shape. The universal approximation theorem says any continuous function can be represented by a network with one hidden layer and enough neurons — but depth buys expressiveness more efficiently than width.
The coloured region is the network's current decision surface. Darker = more confident. As training proceeds the surface bends to fit the data. Weight thickness in the network diagram shows magnitude; teal = positive, red = negative. Neurons that go dim are contributing little (potentially dead).
A flexible network with too little data or too many parameters will memorise the training points, including the noise, and fail to generalise. Increase L2, add dropout, shrink the network, or get more data. The gap between train loss and test accuracy is the clearest signal.
