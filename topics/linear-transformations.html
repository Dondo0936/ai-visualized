The Big Idea
A matrix is a function that transforms space
A 2×2 matrix takes any point (x, y) and moves it somewhere new. It's a function! The two columns tell you where the basis vectors î and ĵ end up. Everything else stretches, rotates, or shears to match.

What's the Determinant?

The determinant tells you how much the transformation scales area. det = 1 means area is preserved. det = 2 means it doubles. det = 0 means space collapses onto a line. Negative det means space is flipped.

Connection to AI

Every layer of a neural network multiplies its input by a weight matrix. Matrix multiplication IS a linear transformation. A 512×512 weight matrix transforms 512D space. Stacking these (plus non-linear activations) lets networks learn any function.
